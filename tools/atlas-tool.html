Hermes Atlas & Avatar Tool

What this is: two utilities for editing Nova's textures and generating her chat avatar.
Why you need it: (1) the texture PNGs are a grid of disassembled parts — ears, blush, mouth, each hair strand — and this tool tells you which pixels belong to which feature. (2) The current chat-box avatar needs to be Nova's actual face, not a placeholder.

Part 1 — Generate Nova's chat avatar

1 Wait for the model to load.

It fetches from /live2d-models/hermes_dark/runtime/… and takes a few seconds. Status will say "model loaded" when ready.

2 Frame the face in the preview.

Use the sliders below the canvas to adjust zoom, x, y until Nova's face is nicely centered. The preview updates live — what you see is what you'll get in the avatar.

3 Click "Render avatar" → "Download".

Render avatar snapshots the current framing to a square PNG. Download saves it as nova.png into your Downloads folder.

Then move it: mv ~/Downloads/nova.png ~/Open-LLM-VTuber/avatars/nova.png
And tell me to update conf.yaml, or do it yourself — change both avatar: lines from nova_stopgap.png to nova.png.

Part 2 — Atlas overlay (see which pixels are which feature)

1 Click "Build atlas overlay".

This fetches all 5 texture PNGs, reads the ArtMesh UV data from the loaded model, and paints each mesh's pixel region on top of the source texture. Takes a couple seconds.

2 Read the atlas maps.

Each texture becomes an annotated map: colored overlays show where each ArtMesh's pixels live. Labels on top name the mesh (e.g. ArtMesh_Hair_Front, ArtMesh_Blush_L).

3 Isolate one feature in the legend below.

The legend lists every mesh by name. Click a name to hide its overlay — makes it easy to isolate just the ears, or just the blush, or just a specific hair strand. "Hide all" then click-to-enable lets you pick one mesh at a time.

Use alpha slider for visibility balance, stroke only for just outlines (so the texture shows through unobscured), labels to toggle text.
